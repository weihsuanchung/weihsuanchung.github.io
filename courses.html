Quantum Algorithm for Solving PDEs and Its Applications

Quantum Information and Computation · 2025 Spring · Lecturer: Prof. Hao-Chung Cheng (NTU EE)
  • Surveyed three approaches to quantum PDEs: (i) wave-equation Hamiltonian simulation (log qubits), (ii) advection via block-encoded nonunitary steps with postselection reuse, (iii) high-order finite-difference mappings to banded Hamiltonians.
  • Analyzed space/time advantages vs. classical solvers; discussed boundary handling, state prep, Trotter error.

We highlighted qubitization and improved observable extraction as promising next steps toward practical quantum PDE solvers.

Regression and Bayesian Inference: Mean Squared Error

Physical Theories on (Machine) Learning · 2025 Fall · Lecturer: Prof. Miranda C. N. Cheng (NTU Physics)
  • Linked MSE minimization to maximum likelihood under Gaussian noise.
  • Showed MAP with Gaussian prior recovers ridge regression with penalty strength \(\lambda=\sigma^2/\tau^2\).

Bridges statistical physics intuition with Bayesian regression, clarifying priors, likelihood, and regularization.

Exact Decoding of Quantum Error-Correcting Codes

IAMS Group Meeting / Coding Theory · 2025 Fall · Presented at Jen Group (IAMS, Academia Sinica)
  • Explored exact decoding algorithms for QEC; performance and scalability trade-offs.
  • Motivated by Prof. Chun-Ming Chen’s Coding Theory course; based on PRL 134, 190603.

Connected coding-theoretic decoding with physical error models, emphasizing regimes where exact methods are viable.

The Features of Optical Computing

Introduction to Semiconductor Optoelectronics · 2024 Spring · Lecturer: Prof. Yun-Chorng Chang (RCAS, Academia Sinica)
  • Introduced photonic computing with visible/IR photons for digital computation.
  • Discussed advantages: spatial parallelism, low-loss links, low cross-talk, one-way propagation, very high carrier bandwidth (~500 THz).

Outlined key hurdles: co-designing optical processor architectures, advancing photonic materials, reducing system bulk.

Comparing Gate-Based vs. Unitary-Based Pulse Generation with Qiskit Pulse (in progress)

Introduction to Quantum Computation and Information · 2025 Fall · Lecturer: Prof. Hsi-Sheng Guan (NTU Physics)
  • Gate-based pulses: predefined/optimized for standard gates; Unitary-based: custom operations and flexibility.

Highlights the trade-off between ease-of-use and control customization in practical quantum pulse synthesis.
